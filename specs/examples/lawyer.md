# Project Specification

## Project
### Name
Marin & Vale Immigration Law

### Slug
marin-vale-immigration-law

### Industry
Immigration law firm for family-based, humanitarian, and business immigration matters

## Business Goals
### Primary Goal
Generate qualified consultation requests from individuals and families seeking immigration legal help in South Florida.

### Secondary Goals

- Establish the firm as experienced, credible, and compassionate.
- Clarify the practice areas so prospects can identify the right legal service quickly.
- Support local search visibility for immigration attorney and immigration lawyer queries.
- Build trust through attorney bios, case approach, multilingual support, and FAQ content.

## Technical Requirements
### Framework
Astro

### Database
None for launch. The site should remain ready for future consultation intake tracking and CRM integration.

### Languages
English and Spanish

### Deployment
Static-first deployment with support for multilingual content and reliable lead-form delivery.

## Pages

- Home
  - Purpose: Position the firm, explain who it helps, and convert qualified prospects.
  - Key sections: Hero, firm overview, practice area summary, trust indicators, attorney spotlight, testimonials, consultation CTA.
  - Primary CTA: Schedule a consultation.

- About the Firm
  - Purpose: Explain the firm's values, approach, and legal background.
  - Key sections: Firm story, legal philosophy, client service approach, languages spoken, bar admissions.
  - Primary CTA: Learn why clients choose the firm.

- Practice Areas
  - Purpose: Provide a clear overview of legal services.
  - Key sections: Family petitions, adjustment of status, waivers, asylum-related support, business immigration, removal defense overview.
  - Primary CTA: Find your immigration matter.

- Family Immigration
  - Purpose: Capture high-intent family-based immigration leads.
  - Key sections: Case types, process overview, common challenges, expected documentation, consultation CTA.
  - Primary CTA: Discuss your family case.

- Deportation Defense
  - Purpose: Address urgent matters with clear guidance.
  - Key sections: Situations handled, urgency guidance, representation approach, court-related support, FAQ.
  - Primary CTA: Request urgent legal help.

- Attorneys
  - Purpose: Build trust with lawyer credentials and experience.
  - Key sections: Attorney bios, education, languages, representative experience, professional memberships.
  - Primary CTA: Meet the legal team.

- FAQ
  - Purpose: Reduce friction by answering common consultation questions.
  - Key sections: Consultation expectations, documents to bring, fee discussion approach, case timelines, language support.
  - Primary CTA: Prepare for your consultation.

- Contact
  - Purpose: Capture consultation inquiries and provide office access details.
  - Key sections: Consultation request form, phone number, office address, office hours, map, parking and building access notes.
  - Primary CTA: Call or request a consultation.

## Features

- Consultation request form with practice-area selection.
- Prominent click-to-call action on mobile.
- Attorney profile content with credentials and languages spoken.
- FAQ page for pre-consultation education.
- Testimonial and trust-indicator sections across high-intent pages.
- Bilingual content support for English and Spanish.

## SEO
### Local SEO

- Target immigration lawyer and immigration attorney searches in Miami, Doral, and Hialeah.
- Maintain consistent business name, office address, phone number, and hours across all contact references.
- Use localized legal-service language without making outcome guarantees.
- Reinforce local authority through attorney credentials, office information, and practice-area specificity.

### Metadata

- Every public page must have unique metadata aligned with the legal intent of the page.
- Home page metadata should emphasize immigration law representation in South Florida.
- Practice area pages must target service-specific search behavior such as family immigration and deportation defense.
- Social metadata should present the firm name and consultation-driven value proposition.

### Sitemap

- Include all public marketing and practice area pages.
- Exclude future form-confirmation or internal resource pages from indexing.

### Schema.org

- Use `Attorney` or `LegalService` as the primary business schema.
- Use `FAQPage` on the FAQ page.
- Use `Person` markup for attorney profiles if implemented later.

## Content

- Voice should be professional, empathetic, direct, and credible.
- Home page must explain who the firm helps and which immigration matters it handles.
- Practice area content must clarify scope without offering legal guarantees or oversimplified promises.
- Attorney bios must include education, admissions, language fluency, and relevant experience.
- FAQ content must prepare leads for the consultation process and required documentation.
- Spanish content must read like native client-facing legal marketing, not literal translation.
- Contact content must explain how quickly the firm responds to new consultation requests and what information prospects should provide.

## Integrations

- Google Maps embed for office location.
- Google Analytics for lead-source visibility.
- Form submission delivery to the firm's intake email inbox.

## Acceptance Criteria

- All required pages exist and include the defined key sections.
- Prospective clients can identify the correct practice area and reach a consultation action within one or two page interactions.
- Attorney credentials and languages spoken are visible in the About or Attorneys content.
- Content avoids legal guarantees while still explaining the firm's service scope clearly.
- Metadata and local SEO positioning support immigration-law discovery in Miami, Doral, and Hialeah.
- English and Spanish content are both complete if bilingual support is presented.
