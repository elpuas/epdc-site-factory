# Content Skill

## Purpose

Define the implementation guidance for structuring content in EPDC projects so pages are clear, conversion-oriented, locally relevant, and aligned with the project specification.

This skill documents how future content work should be organized before or alongside implementation.

## Content Structure

- Start from the specification, not from generic marketing templates.
- Give each page one clear purpose that supports the sitemap and business goals.
- Organize content in a scannable order: context, value, proof, details, action.
- Use short sections with strong headings instead of dense undifferentiated blocks.
- Match the content depth to user intent; high-intent pages need more operational detail than overview pages.
- Ensure trust-building elements appear near decision points, not only at the bottom of the page.

## Page Hierarchy

- Home page should summarize the business, core services, differentiators, and primary next step.
- About pages should establish credibility, philosophy, and human identity.
- Service pages should explain what the service is, who it is for, why it matters, and what the next step is.
- Contact pages should remove friction by surfacing business details and direct contact actions.
- Resource or FAQ pages should answer repeated questions that block conversion or confidence.
- High-intent pages should receive more structured detail than broad overview pages.

## Calls To Action

- Each page should have one primary CTA aligned with the page purpose.
- Secondary CTAs may exist, but they must not compete with the primary conversion action.
- CTA language should be specific, such as `Request an appointment`, `Schedule a consultation`, or `Call the clinic`.
- Place CTAs near the top of decision-critical pages and repeat them at logical points further down.
- Ensure CTA wording matches the business model and user expectation.
- Avoid vague CTA text such as `Submit` or `Learn more` when a stronger alternative is possible.

## Service Pages

- Start with a clear service overview written for a non-expert audience.
- Explain who the service is for and what problem it addresses.
- Include practical expectations such as process, timing, required materials, or next steps when relevant.
- Add trust signals such as practitioner experience, common concerns, or FAQs where the specification supports them.
- Keep service pages focused on one service intent whenever SEO or conversion depends on that clarity.
- End with a CTA that fits the service decision, such as consultation, appointment request, or direct call.

## Local Business Content

- Reflect the actual service area, location, and business details defined in the specification.
- Use local references naturally where they help users understand availability or relevance.
- Make business name, address, phone number, hours, and location guidance consistent across pages.
- Support local trust with concrete business information, testimonials, credentials, and practical visit details.
- Do not fabricate neighborhood coverage, office history, or review language.

## Multilingual Content

- Offer multilingual content only when the specification requires it.
- Treat each language version as a complete user experience, not a partial mirror.
- Preserve CTA meaning, trust signals, and operational details across languages.
- Adapt phrasing for natural readability rather than literal sentence-by-sentence translation.
- Keep page purpose and hierarchy aligned across language versions even when wording changes.
- Ensure business-critical facts remain consistent in every language.

## EPDC Standards

- Content must map directly to the specification and planned page structure.
- Primary conversion pages should combine clarity, trust, and a visible next step.
- Service pages should follow repeatable patterns without becoming generic.
- Local-business content should be specific, accurate, and operationally useful.
- Multilingual support must be complete wherever promised.
- Content structure should help both users and future SEO work understand the site quickly.
