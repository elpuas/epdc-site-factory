# Dental Platform Market Research

## Source Boundary

The findings in this document are derived from the provided research summary for Costa Rican dental websites.

This document does not claim direct access to individual websites, does not add unsupported research, and does not reference competitor names.

Its purpose is to convert the supplied summary into reusable platform findings.

## Common Components

The provided research summary indicates that Costa Rican dental websites commonly rely on the following shared sections:

| Component | Reusable finding | Platform implication |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Header with logo, menu, and phone | Visitors need fast identity, navigation, and contact access | Treat header and visible phone number as baseline platform elements |
| Hero with dental image and CTA | First impression and immediate action matter | The home page needs a strong top-of-page conversion block |
| Services or specialties section | Clinics must quickly explain treatment scope | Service overview content should appear early in the page |
| About or clinic section | Trust depends on humanizing the practice | The platform should support clinic story, care philosophy, and environment messaging |
| Locations or branches | Multi-location clarity can influence choice | The platform must support one or multiple locations cleanly |
| Doctors or professional team | Professional identity is a trust driver | Doctor presentation should be a first-class content type |
| Testimonials or Google reviews | Social proof is a recurring persuasive layer | Review content should be reusable across home and dedicated trust pages |
| Membership or association logos | Professional credibility is visual as well as textual | Trust-logo support should exist in data and components |
| Benefits or differentiators | Clinics compete on reassurance, not only treatments | Differentiator content should sit near service and CTA blocks |
| Contact or appointment form | Sites need a direct conversion surface | Appointment conversion should not depend on one page only |
| Blog or educational section | SEO and patient education are closely linked | Blog structure should be part of the canonical dental platform |
| Footer with contact and social links | Users expect closing navigation and contact confirmation | Footer content should repeat key contact and navigation data |

## Common CTAs

The research summary indicates two primary action patterns:

- `Agendar cita`
- `Escribir por WhatsApp`

These suggest a dual-CTA model:

- primary CTA for appointment intent
- secondary CTA for low-friction messaging

Phone contact also remains important, especially in the header and contact surfaces.

## Common Trust Signals

The research summary points to several recurring forms of trust communication:

- medical credibility
- years of experience
- number of patients treated
- certified specialists
- modern technology
- family-friendly care
- payment flexibility
- visible reviews

These signals should be treated as configurable content blocks, not hardcoded design decoration.

## Common Navigation Patterns

The research summary suggests a practical navigation model centered on:

- home
- services or specialties
- about the clinic
- doctors or team
- locations or contact
- blog

This points to a compact top-level information architecture that prioritizes conversion, trust, and discoverability over deep content trees.

## Common Service-Page Needs

Even though the summary focuses on architectural patterns rather than full service-page breakdowns, the observed emphasis on services and specialties implies that service pages should support:

- treatment overview
- patient-fit or candidacy explanation
- benefits
- trust reinforcement
- next-step CTA
- FAQ support for search and reassurance

## Blog Usage

The research summary shows blog or educational content appearing repeatedly.

This implies the blog is not optional decoration. It serves at least two platform roles:

- support local and treatment-intent SEO
- help patients understand treatments before contacting the clinic

The platform should therefore reserve content and data structures for article lists, article metadata, and treatment-adjacent internal linking.

## WhatsApp Usage

The summary identifies WhatsApp as the main action channel in Costa Rica.

That has direct architectural implications:

- WhatsApp should be a persistent secondary CTA
- mobile-first WhatsApp access should be easy
- WhatsApp should appear in the hero, repeated CTA areas, and floating action patterns when appropriate
- the platform should treat WhatsApp as structured contact data, not as ad hoc page copy

## Location Strategy

The summary shows location content as important, especially for clinics with branches.

That means the platform should support:

- a single primary clinic
- multiple branches with consistent schema
- maps and hours
- branch-specific contact details when needed

Location content should be usable on the home page, contact page, and future location-specific landing pages.

## Doctor And Team Presentation

Doctor and specialist content appears repeatedly in the summary because dental decisions are often trust-led rather than brand-led.

The platform should support:

- lead doctor profile
- additional specialists
- credentials
- areas of focus
- clinic role
- optional photo and trust indicators

Doctor content should support both page sections and future dedicated profile pages if needed.

## Review Strategy

Testimonials and Google reviews appear as recurring social proof.

The platform should support:

- curated testimonial excerpts
- rating or source attribution when approved
- optional Google review integration later
- reuse of review data across home, testimonials, and service-adjacent trust blocks

Review placement should reinforce moments of hesitation, especially after service explanation and before strong appointment CTAs.

## Payment And Financing Messaging

The research summary identifies payment flexibility as a commercial theme.

That suggests the platform should support structured messaging for:

- payment methods
- installment or financing messaging
- insurance acceptance where relevant

This content matters both for trust and for conversion because treatment affordability can be a blocking question before contact.
