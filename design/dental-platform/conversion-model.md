# Dental Platform Conversion Model

## Conversion Goal

The dental platform should reduce friction between interest and appointment action.

For Costa Rican dental clinics, the provided research summary indicates that visitors respond strongly to:

- WhatsApp access
- visible phone contact
- clear service presentation
- doctor credibility
- location certainty
- reviews and reassurance

The conversion model should therefore prioritize speed, trust, and clarity over complex lead flows.

## Primary CTA

Recommended primary CTA:

- `Agendar cita`

Why it exists:

- It aligns with direct appointment intent.
- It gives the clinic one explicit commercial goal.
- It works across home, service, doctor, and contact pages.

## Secondary CTA

Recommended secondary CTA:

- `Escribir por WhatsApp`

Why it exists:

- The source research highlights WhatsApp as the main action channel in Costa Rica.
- Some patients are not ready to complete a form but are willing to message.
- It lowers friction for mobile visitors and first-contact questions.

## WhatsApp Usage

Recommended platform behavior:

- include WhatsApp in the hero
- keep it visible in repeated CTA areas
- support a floating WhatsApp button where appropriate
- use prefilled message intent when useful

Why it exists:

- It matches local user behavior from the provided research summary.
- It shortens the path from interest to conversation.
- It supports patients with urgent or high-anxiety questions.

## Appointment Flow

Recommended flow:

1. Discover clinic through home, service, or blog page.
2. Build confidence through services, doctors, reviews, and trust content.
3. Present appointment CTA after the first trust layer and again after deeper content.
4. Offer WhatsApp, phone, and contact form as parallel action paths.
5. Confirm location and hours before the final contact decision.

Why it exists:

- Dental decisions often require reassurance before commitment.
- The site should progressively remove objections without forcing a single interaction mode.

## Phone Visibility

Recommended platform behavior:

- show phone in the header
- repeat phone in contact and footer sections
- keep mobile click-to-call accessible

Why it exists:

- Phone remains a high-intent channel for appointment-ready visitors.
- Visible phone contact reinforces legitimacy and accessibility.

## Repeated CTAs

Recommended CTA placements:

- hero
- after featured services
- after reviews or trust sections
- near location or contact sections
- footer

Why it exists:

- Not every visitor decides at the same moment.
- Repeated CTAs reduce the need to scroll back or navigate away.

## Trust Placement

Recommended trust placements:

- near the hero through highlights
- after clinic intro
- adjacent to doctor sections
- before strong appointment CTA blocks

Why it exists:

- Trust is a prerequisite to dental conversion.
- Visitors often need reassurance before sharing contact details or booking.

## Review Placement

Recommended review placements:

- on the home page before a strong CTA
- near service trust blocks
- on a dedicated testimonials page

Why it exists:

- Reviews reduce uncertainty at the decision stage.
- Patient proof complements doctor and clinic claims.

## Map Placement

Recommended map placements:

- contact page
- home page snapshot for primary location
- multi-branch sections when relevant

Why it exists:

- Dental visits are location-sensitive.
- Visitors need confidence that the clinic is practical to reach.

## Contact Strategy

Recommended contact model:

- appointment-focused contact page
- visible WhatsApp and phone throughout the site
- form for structured inquiries
- map, hours, and parking guidance on the contact surface

Why it exists:

- Conversion fails when logistics remain unclear.
- A strong dental contact page should remove practical blockers, not only collect leads.

## Costa Rica-Specific Commercial Logic

This model is tuned for Costa Rica because the source research summary indicates that the most repeatable commercial pattern is:

WhatsApp + services + doctors + location + reviews + blog SEO

That means the future blueprint should not over-index on abstract branding alone. It should help clinics:

- get contacted quickly
- explain treatments clearly
- show who provides the care
- prove where the clinic is
- reinforce confidence with reviews
- capture search demand with educational content
